### Implementation Notes for gas_thermal.py

The gas temperature equation source term from reaction enthalpy:

```
Q_rxn = - Σ_j (ΔH_j × R_j)    [W/m³]
```

where:
- ΔH_j = reaction enthalpy [J/mol] (from this table × 1000)
- R_j = reaction rate [mol/(m³·s)]
- Negative sign: exothermic (ΔH < 0) → Q_rxn > 0 (heats gas)

**Important considerations:**
1. Tier 3 reactions (EI, ionization, attachment) should NOT be included — 
   their energy comes from the electron energy equation, not gas thermal.
2. For DR reactions, the kinetic energy of products goes to gas heating.
3. For neutral reactions, full ΔH goes to gas.
4. For charge transfer, the exothermicity goes partly to internal energy
   of the product ion — simplified assumption: all to gas.
